Homi Jehangir
Bhabha

1909-1966

We honor Dr. Homi Bhabha — a visionary pioneer, a devoted patriot, and one of the most brilliant scientific minds India has ever gifted to the world.

Dr. Homi Jehangir Bhabha was a visionary physicist who laid the foundation for India’s scientific and nuclear future. Educated at Cambridge, he made key contributions to quantum mechanics before returning to India with a mission to build a modern, self-reliant nation. In 1945, he founded the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR), followed by the Atomic Energy Commission in 1948, spearheading India’s peaceful nuclear program. Bhabha believed in using atomic energy for progress—in power, medicine, and agriculture. Beyond science, he was a lover of the arts and a cultured leader whose legacy continues to inspire. His untimely death in 1966 was a national loss, but his vision lives on in every stride India makes in science and technology.
